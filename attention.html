Attention

Smartphones

  • Average smartphone users pick up their phone 27 times per day (14-150 depending on the study)
  • Those who have owned one for longer check it much more frequently
  • 42% check their phone when they have time to kill
  • 23% claim to do so only when there is something specific to do

Smartphones Part 2

  • While driving their cars: 55%
  • In a movie theater: 35%
  • On a dinner date: 33%
  • Attending a child's school function: 32%
  • At church: 19%
  • In the shower: 12%
  • During sex: 10%

Instant Gratification & the Internet

  • Study from UMass-Amherst collected server data from 23 million online video views
  • Most viewers abandoned the video if it didn't load with 2 seconds
  • With a 10 second delay, 2/3 of viewers left the screen for another source of information.
  • We expect instant gratification.

Task-switching

  • A recent study followed a group of younger and older adults who were glasses with embedded cameras for 300 hours
  • Younger adults: switched tasks 27 times per hour (or once every 2 minutes).
  • Older adults: 17 times per hour (once every 3-4 minutes).

Definition

The process by which the mind chooses among the various stimuli that strike the senses at any given moment. It allows only some information to enter into consciousness.

My experience is what I agree to attend to. - William James

Top down

Internal guidance of attention based on prior knowledge, willful plans, and current goals.

Bottom up

Attentional guidance purely by externally driven factors to stimuli that are salient.

Attention can be trained.

Focused attention

Sustaining selective attention moment by moment on a chosen object, such as a subset of localized sensations caused by respiration.

Focused Attention

  • Directing and sustaining attention on selected object
  • Detecting mind wandering and distractors
  • Shifting of attention back to the selected object

Gamma Synchrony Study (Lutz et al, 2004)

  • Group 1: 8 long-term Buddhist meditators, Group 2: 10 novice meditators
  • Recorded EEG before and during meditation
  • Question: Do the long-term practioners show greater gamma synchrony during meditation?

Suggests that long-term meditation:

  • Increases (sustained) gamma activity
  • Increases synchrony between hemispheres
  • More meditation experience = stronger effect
  • "Our study is consistent with the idea that attention and affective processes, which gamma-band EEG synchronization may reflect, are flexible skills that can be trained"

Expert meditator study

  • Group 1: Focused Attention Experts, Group 2:Novices
  • "Focus on a visual cue while we scan your brain and play annoying sounds"
  • Question: Does FA meditation change your brain response to distraction?

Decrease in brain response to distraction.

EMBODY study (Weng et al, under review)

  • 8 Meditators from the Vipassana and Zen traditions
  • Average lifetime meditation practice in meditators was 3495 hours (range 509-6590)
  • Question: We we detect in real-time whether you are engaging in meditative practice?

EMBODY Study

  • With modern brain imaging methods, its now possible to decode meditative states in real-time
  • While all participants spent the majority of time attending to breath (vs. mind wandering or self-referential processing), meditators with more lifetime practice demonstrated greater overall breath attention.
  • Question: is this possible with EEG?
