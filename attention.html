Attention

Definition

The process by which the mind chooses among the various stimuli that strike the senses at any given moment. It allows only some information to enter into consciousness.

My experience is what I agree to attend to. - William James

Top down

Internal guidance of attention based on prior knowledge, willful plans, and current goals.

Bottom up

Attentional guidance purely by externally driven factors to stimuli that are salient.

Focused attention

Sustaining selective attention moment by moment on a chosen object, such as a subset of localized sensations caused by respiration.

Focused Attention

  • Directing and sustaining attention on selected object
  • Detecting mind wandering and distractors
  • Shifting of attention back to the selected object
