Perception can be trained.

Open Monitoring

One aims to remain only in the monitoring state, attentive moment by moment to anything that occurs in experience without focusing on any explicit object.

Open Monitoring

  • no explicit focus on objects
  • no labeling of experience
  • non-reactive awareness of automatic cognitive and emotional reactions

Pain perception study

  • Group 1: Focused Attention, Group 2: Open Monitoring
  • "Cold pressor" task
  • Question: Are there differences in how people respond to pain depending on the type of meditation?

Decrease in pain unpleasantness (but not intensity) with OM meditation

Short-term meditation study

  • Group 1: Control Group 2: Integrative body–mind training
  • 40 undergrads, over 3 months
  • No effort to control thoughts, but instead a state of restful alertness that allows a high degree of awareness of body and breathing

Short-term meditation study
